Bellecour square

Bellecour Square

Bellecour square is the most important square in Lyon and the fourth largest square in France with a size of 310 by 200 meters, or approximately 62,000 m2.

Its dimensions exceed those of Mexico City Zocalo (44,160 m2 and Moscow's Red Square (23,100 m2). It is also the largest pedestrian square in Europe, vehicles circulate around the square. In the center of the square there is a bronze statue of Louis XIV.

Place Bellecour today

Place Bellecour today This square is located between the Rhône and the Saône rivers and in the second arrondissement of Lyon. It constitutes the zero kilometer point: all distances are counted from this point.

It also sets a reference altitude for the city, 170 meters. From this square start three major axes of the peninsula , two of which are pedestrianized: Républic street which leads to the Town Hall and the Opera, Victor Hugo street which leads to Perrache and Président Edouard Herriot street which leads to Terreaux square.

Story

Story

The square has known several names: Louis-le-Grand square, Fédération square, Egality square, Royale square and finally Bonaparte square or even Bella Curtis. Despite what one might think, Bellecour square was born relatively late in the history of Lyon, although it carries an undeniable influence. In this, it follows the history of public places well.
